Ministers of India

Ministers of India project analyses the publicly available orders, pertaining to appointment of ministers to various ministries. These orderscan be found on the Cabinet Secretariat's website.

Orders

There are two kinds of orders found of the Cabinet Secretariat's website that are analyzed
  1. Change of portfolio: Order pertaining to assignment of reqlinuishmen of a particular minister to a ministry.
  2. Council of Ministers: At a regular interval the Cabinet Secretariat isuses an order consisting of council of ministers and their ministries on that particular day. This is the assignment on the day the order was issued.

The time span for these orders is 75 years, starting from the first order on 15th August 1947, that formed India's first cabinet to the latest order that shows the current council of ministers and their ministries.

It this project we analyze over 990 orders, which consist of over 2,500 pages and approx 0.5 million words. Almost all these orders are images (image pdfs) of the orders that were issued over the period of about 75 years.

Analysis

The analysis consists of building the following entites from the information found in the orders
  1. Order Details From every order we extract an entity called an Order Detail. An order detail consists of all the infomation metioned about a single minister in tha particular order. The order detail consists of the name and identification number(ID) of the minister along with the name and IDs for all the ministries that minister is associated with, i.e, all the ministers that minister is assuming, relinquishing or continuing with, along with the rank of the Minister (Cabinet Minister or Minister of State). Every order can be thought of as made up of several Order Details. The order detail captures all the key information pertaining to the minister and their ministries.
  2. Tenures After building order details from the order, the next entity that is built is the Tenure. A tenure has a start date and an end-date and is associated with a one Minister and one Ministry, it also consists of all the orders that match the particular tenure. It is worth noting that the Order detail consits of multiple ministries mentioned in the order for a particular minister, whereas a Tenure is associate with a single minister and a single minstry (post) over a period of time. A minister could be involved with multiple tenures on a given date, as he/she could be holding multiple posts.
  3. Org Chart The last entity we build is the org chart. For every tenure we can indentify its managerial and reportee tenures. For example if a tenure of a minister is for Home Ministry from date1 to date 2, then durping that ternure the managerial tenure will be the tenure of teh Prime Minister, as cabinet minister reports to the prime minister. Similarly the reportee tenures will be those tenure that correspond to the Home ministry, of rank "Minister of State" and are active during the same period. This list of manager and repotee tenures for each tenures allows us to build a current Org Chart as well as an Org Chart at an earlier time.
